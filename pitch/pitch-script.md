# Impact Intelligence — Pitch Script (~2.5 min)

---

## 1. Hook (~10s)

> Oil prices shifted overnight. A new tariff dropped. A monsoon warning just hit.
>
> What does any of that mean for *your* commute, your grocery bill, or your business?
>
> You shouldn't need to be an economist to answer that.

---

## 2. Problem (~20s)

News tells you *what* happened — not what it means for *you*.

A commuter in Mumbai, a farmer in Punjab, and a shop owner in Bengaluru all see the same headline — but the impact on their lives is completely different. And there's no way to tell if what you're reading is verified fact or speculation.

**The information exists. The understanding doesn't.**

---

## 3. Solution (~30s)

**Impact Intelligence** turns live global signals into persona-first, actionable need cards.

We ingest from Apify (Google News), U.S. EIA (oil prices), and ReliefWeb (humanitarian data). Gemini reframes each signal for five personas — commuter, student, SMB owner, farmer, importer — with tailored summaries and recommended actions.

Every card answers three questions: **What happened? Why does it matter to me? What should I do?**

And every claim carries a confidence badge and full source citations — so you always know what's fact and what's inference.

---

## 4. Demo (~40s)

> *[Live app: impact-cursor-hackathon.vercel.app]*

- **Feed** — Scroll through need cards, each showing persona, urgency, confidence, and a recommended action. Local context appears for Indian cities.
- **Filter** — Switch persona to "Commuter," category to "Energy & Fuel," region to "Mumbai" — the feed reshapes in real time.
- **Detail** — Click into a card: "What We Know" vs. "What We Infer" sections, India impact narrative, per-persona actions, and a citations panel with publisher, date, and source type.
- **Resilience** — Every API is independently optional. Remove any key — the app degrades gracefully, never breaks.

---

## 5. Differentiators (~20s)

Three things set us apart:

1. **Persona-first framing** — the same event produces different cards for a commuter vs. a farmer. Nobody else does this.
2. **Trust as a first-class feature** — confidence badges separate fact from inference, with a full citation trail on every claim.
3. **City-level local context** — not national averages, but notes for Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, and beyond.

---

## 6. Impact (~20s)

India has 130M+ daily commuters, 63M+ small businesses, and 118M+ farming households — all affected by global events, none of them well-served by generic news.

Impact Intelligence closes that gap. People stop reacting and start preparing. A farmer in rural Maharashtra gets the same quality of intelligence as a trader in South Mumbai.

This full-stack app — pipeline, AI layer, feed, citations, accessibility — was built in a hackathon sprint with Cursor.
